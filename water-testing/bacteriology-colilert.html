Bacteriology (Colilert)

Presence/absence (100 mL sample).

Before sampling

  • Run the tap for 10 minutes before sampling.
  • Sanitize the outlet with alcohol swab or 1:10 bleach/water.
  • Use aseptic technique (don’t touch the inside of cap/bottle).

You will need

  • Sterile 120 mL sample bottle (non-fluorescing vessel)
  • Colilert reagent packet (for 100 mL sample)
  • Comparator (colour/fluorescence reference)
  • Incubator set to ~35–36°C
  • 365 nm UV lamp (long-wave)
  • Marker/label

Procedure

  1. Label bottle with location, date, and time.
  2. Open bottle without touching the inside surfaces.
  3. Fill to the 100 mL fill line.
  4. Add one Colilert reagent packet to the bottle.
  5. Cap and mix by inverting/shaking (powder does not have to fully dissolve).
  6. Incubate 24 hours at ~35–36°C.
  7. Read results at 24 hours (do not interpret new positives after 28 hours).
Record results in the Sick Bay water treatment logbook.
Reading results ▾
  • Colourless = negative (total coliforms and E. coli).
  • Yellow (equal to or greater than comparator) = total coliforms present.
  • If yellow: check for blue fluorescence under 365 nm UV in a dark area.
  • Fluorescence equal to or greater than comparator = E. coli present.
  • Tip: use a 6W, 365 nm UV light within ~5 inches of the sample; point the light away from your eyes.
Timing notes ▾
  • If clearly positive before 24 hours, it’s a valid positive.
  • If incubated past 28 hours: colourless remains a valid negative; a yellow colour appearing after 28 hours is not valid and should be repeated/verified.

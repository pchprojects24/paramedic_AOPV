pH

Step-by-step (DR900).

Before sampling

  • Run the tap for 10 minutes before sampling.
  • Install the instrument cap on the DR900 cell holder before pressing ZERO or READ.
  • For accurate results, sample temperature should be 21–29°C.

You will need

  • DR900
  • Two sample cells (with caps)
  • Phenol Red Indicator Solution
  • Dropper (0.5 and 1.0 mL marks)
  • Clean cloth/wipe

Procedure

  1. Start the DR900 program for pH (Program 461 pH).
  2. Prepare the blank: Fill a sample cell with 10 mL of sample.
  3. Clean the blank cell, insert it, then press ZERO (display shows 6.0 pH).
  4. Prepare the sample: Fill a second sample cell with 10 mL of sample.
  5. Use a dropper to add 1.0 mL Phenol Red Indicator Solution to the prepared sample.
  6. Close the sample cell and invert two times to mix.
  7. Clean the prepared sample cell, insert it, then press READ.
  8. Record the result.
Record results in the Sick Bay water treatment logbook.
Notes ▾
  • Expected method range: 6.5–8.5 pH.
  • Salt water (seawater) interferes and cannot be analyzed with this method.

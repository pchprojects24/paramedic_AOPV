Total Chlorine

Step-by-step (DR900).

Before sampling

  • Run the tap for 10 minutes before sampling.
  • Test locations must include the farthest distribution point.

You will need

  • DR900
  • Glass sample cell (10 mL)
  • Total chlorine reagent powder pillow (10 mL)
  • Clean cloth/wipe
  • Timer (3 minutes)

Procedure

  1. Turn on the DR900. Go to Options and select the chlorine program.
  2. Rinse the sample cell, then fill to the 10 mL line with the water sample.
  3. Wipe the outside with a clean cloth. Insert the cell and place the instrument cover over the sample.
  4. Press ZERO (screen should display 0.00).
  5. Add the DPD Total Chlorine reagent (10 mL pouch) and mix until dissolved.
  6. Start a 3-minute timer. When complete, proceed to the next step.
  7. Wipe the outside with a clean cloth. Insert the sample, place the cover over the sample, and press READ.
  8. Record the result. A 1 ppm total chlorine value is a target; if below, action is required to achieve minimum standards.
Record results in the Sick Bay water treatment logbook.

Daily Checks

Start-of-day routine so the analyzer is ready for patient use on AOPV.

Analyzer check-in

  1. Power on from the dock; confirm date/time, battery (>50%), and that the analyzer temp is within normal limits.
  2. Run the electronic simulator if the device was transported, bumped, or unused >24h; document PASS/FAIL in the QC log.
  3. Inspect cartridge port and battery contacts for salt or corrosion; wipe exterior with 70% IPA and keep the port dry.
  4. Verify dock/printer power and paper; keep analyzer on charge when idle.

Cartridges & supplies

  • Check fridge temperature log and cartridge expiry dates; do not use pouches with condensation or damaged foil.
  • Stage a small working supply of each cartridge at room temp; label each pouch with the “out of fridge” date (max 14 days unless manufacturer states otherwise).
  • Ensure QC ampoules for each lot are present and in date; keep the electronic simulator with the analyzer.
  • Stock heparinized syringes/microtainers, lancets, alcohol swabs, gauze, and gloves.

Before first patient

  1. Warm chosen cartridge to room temp for at least 5 minutes while still sealed.
  2. Review QC status on the analyzer; resolve any lockouts before patient care.
  3. Prepare labels/patient ID workflow to ensure results are traceable to the casualty record.

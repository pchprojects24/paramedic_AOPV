Overview

Handheld point-of-care blood analyzer used onboard AOPV for rapid chemistry, blood gas, and cardiac markers.

What it does

  • Runs single-use cartridges (CHEM8+, CG4+, PT/INR, Troponin I) on small whole-blood samples.
  • Gives results in 2–10 minutes for bedside decisions when lab support is limited.
  • Stores, prints, or exports results; supports QC lockouts to enforce quality standards.

Core pieces

  • Handheld analyzer with touchscreen/keys and rechargeable battery (keep docked when not in use).
  • Charging/communication base and optional thermal printer; keep power connected underway.
  • Electronic simulator for daily functional checks and after transport.
  • Liquid QC ampoules matched to cartridge lots; store cold and warm before use.
  • Single-use cartridges kept refrigerated; allow to reach room temp before testing.

Power on & login

  1. Remove from dock, press power, and allow the self-check to complete.
  2. Confirm date/time and analyzer temperature are reasonable; correct if wrong.
  3. Enter operator ID if prompted (use unit’s operator list) to ensure results are attributed.
  4. From the home screen select Analyze (run test), Review (previous results), or System (settings/QC).

Navigation basics

  • Analyze: Insert cartridge when prompted; follow on-screen fill and timing prompts.
  • Review/Print: Reprint or view past results for documentation and trending.
  • System: Check QC status, lot numbers, date/time, comms, and lockouts.
  • Use the on-screen up/down arrows and soft keys to move between menus; Accept confirms choices.

Shut down & care

  • Power down after use or return to the dock to charge; avoid deep discharge.
  • Wipe exterior with 70% IPA; keep the cartridge port dry and free of salt crystals.
  • Store cartridges and QC materials at correct temperatures and log out-of-fridge times.

Cartridge-Related Errors

PLACEHOLDER

Cartridge Not Recognised

  1. Remove the cartridge and inspect the contacts for damage, moisture, or contamination.
  2. Check the cartridge expiry date — do not use expired cartridges.
  3. Verify the cartridge was stored correctly (temperature, sealed pouch).
  4. Try a new cartridge from the same lot.
  5. If the error persists with multiple cartridges, suspect a lot issue or analyser port problem.

Calibration Failure

  1. Verify ambient temperature is 18–30 °C.
  2. Confirm cartridge has been equilibrated to room temperature for at least 5 minutes.
  3. Try a new cartridge.
  4. If persistent, try a cartridge from a different lot.
  5. Run EQC to verify analyser function.

Cartridge Fill Errors

  1. Ensure the sample was applied correctly — fill to the line, no air bubbles.
  2. Close the cartridge cover promptly after sample application.
  3. Verify the sample is not clotted.
  4. Redraw and retry with a new cartridge.

Expired or Improperly Stored Cartridges

Caution: Cartridges exposed to temperatures outside their storage range, or used past their expiry date, may produce inaccurate results or error codes. Always check before use.

Analyser-Related Errors

PLACEHOLDER

Analyser Does Not Power On

  1. Replace the 9 V lithium battery.
  2. Check battery contacts for corrosion — clean with a dry cloth if needed.
  3. If still unresponsive, the analyser may have a hardware fault — escalate.

Display Errors or Garbled Screen

  1. Remove and reinsert the battery (power cycle).
  2. If the display remains abnormal after power cycle, the analyser may need repair.

EQC Failure (Electronic Simulator)

  1. Clean the cartridge port contacts with a dry lint-free swab.
  2. Clean the EQC simulator contacts.
  3. Re-run the EQC.
  4. If still fails: try a different EQC simulator (if available).
  5. If all EQC attempts fail, lock out the analyser. See Lockout Procedures.

Persistent Error Codes Across All Cartridge Types

If the same error appears regardless of cartridge type or lot, the analyser itself is likely at fault. Run EQC, clean the port, power cycle. If unresolved, escalate for repair.

Sample-Related Errors

PLACEHOLDER

Insufficient Sample Volume

  1. Verify the minimum sample volume for the cartridge type.
  2. Redraw an adequate sample.
  3. Use a new cartridge — do not re-use a cartridge after a failed fill.

Clotted Sample

  1. Inspect the syringe for visible clots or fibrin strands.
  2. If the sample is clotted, discard and redraw.
  3. Ensure the sample is analysed within the recommended time (typically within 3–10 minutes of draw).
  4. For venous samples, ensure proper mixing with anticoagulant (if applicable).

Air Bubbles in Sample

  1. Expel air bubbles from the syringe before applying sample to the cartridge.
  2. Apply the sample smoothly and steadily to avoid introducing air.
  3. For blood gas samples (CG4+), air exposure can alter PO2 and PCO2 values.
Caution — Blood Gas Samples: Arterial blood gas samples must be analysed as quickly as possible. Prolonged exposure to air or delays in analysis will alter pH, PCO2, and PO2 results.

Haemolysed / Lipaemic / Icteric Samples

These sample conditions may interfere with certain analytes. Refer to the cartridge-specific interference information. If in doubt, note the sample condition on the result report.

Environmental Errors (Temperature / Humidity)

PLACEHOLDER

Temperature Out of Range

ConditionEffectAction
Ambient < 18 °C Cartridge calibration may fail; results unreliable Move analyser and cartridges to a warmer location (18–30 °C). Allow equilibration before testing.
Ambient > 30 °C Cartridge reagents may degrade; sensor drift Move to a cooler, climate-controlled space. Do not test until temperature is in range.
Cartridge cold (from fridge) Fill errors, calibration failure Allow cartridge to reach room temperature (5+ minutes) before use.

Humidity Issues

  • High humidity may cause condensation on cartridge contacts — wipe dry before use.
  • Cartridge foil pouches damaged by moisture should not be used.
  • Store cartridge boxes in a dry location; keep pouches sealed until ready to use.

Shipboard-Specific Environmental Challenges

Ship motion, engine room heat, saltwater exposure risk, and rapid temperature changes between compartments — mitigation strategies for each.

Communication / Printer Errors

PLACEHOLDER

IR Communication Failure

  1. Clean both IR windows (analyser and printer) with a dry lint-free wipe.
  2. Ensure the two devices are aligned and within the specified distance.
  3. Remove any obstructions between the IR windows.
  4. Ensure there is no strong ambient light source interfering with IR communication.
  5. Retry the data transfer.

Printer Not Printing

  1. Verify the printer has power and is turned on.
  2. Check for a paper jam or empty paper roll.
  3. Replace paper roll if needed.
  4. Clean the print head.
  5. Test with a self-test print from the printer menu.

Data Not Downloading to External System

If using a data management system or downloader, verify cable connections, software settings, and that the analyser is configured to transmit. Refer to the downloader user manual.

When to Escalate

PLACEHOLDER

Escalation Criteria

  • EQC fails repeatedly after cleaning and port inspection.
  • Liquid QC fails across multiple lots of the same cartridge type.
  • The same error code appears on every test regardless of cartridge type.
  • Physical damage to the analyser (cracked display, damaged port, water ingress).
  • Analyser will not power on after battery replacement.
  • Suspected firmware corruption or menu/display anomalies.

Replacement & Repair Process

PLACEHOLDER

Requesting Repair

  1. Lock out the faulty analyser (label, logbook entry, notification).
  2. Complete a repair request form with serial number, fault description, and troubleshooting summary.
  3. Submit to biomedical engineering / equipment management.
  4. Arrange return shipping if shore-based repair is required.

Requesting a Replacement Analyser

  1. Contact the POCT coordinator and supply chain.
  2. Request a loan or replacement i-STAT 1 analyser.
  3. Upon receipt, configure the replacement (date/time, operator IDs, customisation codes).
  4. Run EQC + full liquid QC (all 3 levels, all cartridge types) before patient use.
  5. Record the new analyser serial number in the equipment register and QC logbook.

Returning a Repaired Analyser to Service

  1. Verify the repair report — confirm fault was identified and corrected.
  2. Check firmware version and configuration settings.
  3. Run EQC — must PASS.
  4. Run liquid QC at all 3 levels on all cartridge types — must PASS.
  5. Record return-to-service in the QC logbook with date, operator, EQC and QC results.
  6. Remove "OUT OF SERVICE" label.

CHEM8+

Rapid metabolic panel for fluids/renal decisions in the Sick Bay or casualty area.

Purpose

  • Electrolytes (Na, K, Cl), BUN/creatinine, glucose, ionized calcium, hematocrit/hemoglobin.
  • Guides dehydration/shock resuscitation, renal perfusion, and med dosing (e.g., K+, Ca²⁺ sensitive drugs).

Supplies

  • CHEM8+ cartridge warmed to room temp (sealed until use).
  • 2–3 mL lithium heparin whole blood via syringe or microtainer; lancet for capillary draws.
  • Alcohol swab, gauze, label/PID, and biohazard waste bag.

Storage & Handling

  • Store refrigerated at 2–8°C. Record expiry and “out-of-fridge” date; typical room-temp limit is 14 days (follow kit insert).
  • Warm sealed cartridge 5–10 minutes; avoid condensation and do not hand-warm aggressively.
  • Discard any pouch with damaged foil or moisture.

QC Workflow

  1. On new shipment/lot, run level 1 and level 3 liquid controls once both cartridge and control are at room temp.
  2. Run the electronic simulator after transport or weekly if idle; record PASS/FAIL.
  3. If a control is outside range, repeat with new control and cartridge; quarantine suspect lot and escalate if it persists.

Patient Sample Workflow

  1. Warm cartridge, label it, and confirm analyzer is ready.
  2. Draw lithium heparin whole blood; mix gently. For capillary, wipe the first drop and collect the second.
  3. Fill to the line in one smooth motion without bubbles, close the cap, and insert within 30 seconds.
  4. Select patient ID/test type; results in ~2 minutes.
  5. Print/log values; trend electrolytes and creatinine against baseline when available.

Common Errors & Fixes

  • Underfill or bubbles: Recollect and use a new cartridge; fill smoothly while keeping cartridge level.
  • Hemolysis (K+ artificially high): Avoid excessive tourniquet time and forceful draws; invert gently.
  • Cold/condensation errors: Allow full warm-up; discard damp pouches.

Notes for Shipboard Use

  • Useful for shock, dehydration, renal perfusion checks, and electrolyte-guided fluid therapy during evacuations.
  • Keep a few room-temp cartridges ready for response but rotate back to cold stock before the 14-day limit.
  • Document cartridge lot, operator, and time in the Sick Bay log for traceability.

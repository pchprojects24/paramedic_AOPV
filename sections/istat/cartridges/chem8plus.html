Purpose & Clinical Indications

PLACEHOLDER

What This Cartridge Tests

The CHEM8+ provides a basic metabolic panel measuring electrolytes (Na, K, Cl, TCO2, iCa), renal function markers (BUN, Creatinine), metabolic status (Glucose), and haematocrit (Hct). Haemoglobin (Hgb) and Anion Gap (AnGap) are calculated values.

Clinical Indications

  • Assessment of fluid and electrolyte balance
  • Renal function screening (BUN, Creatinine)
  • Diabetic emergencies / glucose monitoring
  • Metabolic acidosis / alkalosis assessment (TCO2, AnGap)
  • Hypokalaemia / hyperkalaemia assessment
  • Dehydration assessment
  • Calcium disturbances (iCa)
  • Anaemia screening (Hct, Hgb)
  • Baseline metabolic assessment in trauma / acute illness

Limitations & Interferences

Known interferences for CHEM8+ cartridge: heparin concentration effects on iCa, haemolysis effects on K, lipaemia effects, sample age considerations.

Storage Temperature

Refrigerated: 2–8 °C. May also be stored at room temperature (PLACEHOLDER °C range) for up to PLACEHOLDER days per manufacturer instructions.

Shelf Life & Expiry

Check expiry date printed on each cartridge pouch. Do not use after expiry. Once the foil pouch is opened, use within PLACEHOLDER minutes.

Lot Tracking

Record lot number upon receipt. New lots require liquid QC at all 3 levels before patient use. Maintain a lot tracking log cross-referenced with the QC logbook.

Equilibration Before Use

Caution: Allow cartridge to equilibrate to room temperature (18–30 °C) for at least 5 minutes before use. Do not artificially warm.

QC Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

  1. Verify EQC has passed today.
  2. Check the QC logbook — is liquid QC due for CHEM8+ this week?
  3. If due: run Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 liquid QC per QC procedures.
  4. Compare all 9 measured analytes against the lot-specific acceptable ranges.
  5. Record all results (PASS/FAIL per analyte) in the QC logbook.
  6. If any analyte fails at any level, do NOT run patient samples on CHEM8+ until resolved.
  7. See QC Failure Troubleshooting for corrective actions.

Patient Sample Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

Pre-Analysis

  1. Confirm patient identity using two identifiers.
  2. Verify that EQC has passed and QC is current for CHEM8+.
  3. Gather all supplies (see checklist above).
  4. Remove a CHEM8+ cartridge from its foil pouch; confirm it is at room temperature and in-date.

Sample Collection

  1. Collect a venous or arterial whole blood sample using a lithium heparin syringe or tube.
  2. Mix gently by inversion to ensure anticoagulation (if applicable).
  3. Expel any air bubbles from the syringe.

Running the Test

  1. Apply the blood sample to the CHEM8+ cartridge sample well — fill to the line.
  2. Close the cartridge cover firmly.
  3. Insert the cartridge into the i-STAT analyser until it clicks.
  4. Enter patient ID and operator ID when prompted on the display.
  5. Wait for the analysis to complete (~2 minutes).

Post-Analysis

  1. Read all results on the display.
  2. Print the result ticket via IR printer (if available).
  3. Remove and dispose of the used cartridge in the sharps/biohazard container.
  4. Document all results in the patient's medical record.
  5. Check for any critical values — if present, report immediately (see below).

  5. Document: who was notified, time of notification, and read-back confirmation in the patient record.

Notes for Shipboard Use

PLACEHOLDER

Motion & Vibration

The CHEM8+ cartridge is generally robust during moderate sea states. However, during heavy weather, stabilise the analyser on a flat surface and brace to prevent movement during the 2-minute analysis cycle. Avoid testing during severe motion if possible.

Temperature Considerations

Ensure the medical centre refrigerator maintains 2–8 °C for cartridge storage. Monitor room temperature — tropical deployments may push ambient temperatures above 30 °C if air conditioning is inadequate.

Documentation at Sea

When connectivity is unavailable, ensure all results are recorded on paper (printed ticket and/or handwritten in the patient record). Back up QC logbook entries in case of water damage.

Tip: In a trauma scenario at sea, the CHEM8+ provides the broadest initial metabolic picture. Consider running alongside a CG4+ for a comprehensive acute assessment.

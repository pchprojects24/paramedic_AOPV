Purpose & Clinical Indications

PLACEHOLDER

What This Cartridge Tests

The CG4+ measures arterial or venous blood gas parameters (pH, PCO2, PO2) and Lactate. Calculated values include Base Excess (BE), Bicarbonate (HCO3), Total CO2 (TCO2), and Oxygen Saturation (sO2). Together these provide a comprehensive acid-base and oxygenation profile.

Clinical Indications

  • Assessment of respiratory function (oxygenation, ventilation)
  • Acid-base disturbance evaluation (metabolic/respiratory acidosis or alkalosis)
  • Monitoring ventilated patients
  • Sepsis / shock assessment (lactate as perfusion marker)
  • Trauma assessment — tissue perfusion status
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) monitoring
  • Carbon monoxide / toxic inhalation assessment (with limitations)
  • Drowning / near-drowning assessment

Limitations & Interferences

Air exposure rapidly alters PO2 and PCO2 — sample must be analysed immediately. Heparin concentration may affect results. sO2 is calculated (not measured by co-oximetry) and may be inaccurate in CO poisoning, methaemoglobinaemia, or abnormal haemoglobins.

Caution — Calculated sO2: The CG4+ calculates sO2 from PO2 and pH using a standard dissociation curve. It does NOT directly measure oxygen saturation. In cases of CO poisoning or methaemoglobinaemia, sO2 will be falsely normal. Use a co-oximeter for these cases.

QC Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

  1. Verify EQC has passed today.
  2. Check the QC logbook — is liquid QC due for CG4+ this week?
  3. If due: run Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 liquid QC per QC procedures.
  4. Compare all 4 measured analytes (pH, PCO2, PO2, Lactate) against lot-specific acceptable ranges.
  5. Record all results (PASS/FAIL per analyte) in the QC logbook.
  6. If any analyte fails at any level, do NOT run patient samples on CG4+ until resolved.
  7. See QC Failure Troubleshooting for corrective actions.

Patient Sample Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

Pre-Analysis

  1. Confirm patient identity using two identifiers.
  2. Verify that EQC has passed and QC is current for CG4+.
  3. Gather all supplies (see checklist above).
  4. Remove a CG4+ cartridge from its foil pouch; confirm it is at room temperature and in-date.
Critical — Sample Handling: Blood gas samples are extremely time-sensitive. Analyse within 3 minutes of collection for arterial samples. Minimise air exposure at all times.

Sample Collection (Arterial)

  1. Perform a modified Allen's test (if radial artery puncture).
  2. Clean the puncture site with alcohol; allow to dry.
  3. Perform arterial puncture using a pre-heparinised ABG syringe.
  4. Collect the required volume — typically 1–3 mL.
  5. Immediately remove any air bubbles from the syringe.
  6. Cap the syringe to prevent further air exposure.
  7. Mix gently by rolling between palms.
  8. Apply pressure to the puncture site for at least 5 minutes.

Running the Test

  1. Apply the blood sample to the CG4+ cartridge sample well — fill to the line.
  2. Close the cartridge cover firmly.
  3. Insert the cartridge into the i-STAT analyser until it clicks.
  4. Enter patient ID and operator ID when prompted.
  5. Wait for the analysis to complete (~2 minutes).

Post-Analysis

  1. Read all results on the display — both measured and calculated values.
  2. Print the result ticket via IR printer (if available).
  3. Remove and dispose of the used cartridge in the sharps/biohazard container.
  4. Document all results in the patient's medical record.
  5. Note the sample type (arterial or venous) and any relevant clinical details (FiO2, ventilator settings).
  6. Check for critical values — report immediately if present.

Reporting Procedure

  1. Identify the critical value on the result display.
  2. Immediately contact the responsible medical officer.
  3. Report: patient identity, test, result, sample type (arterial/venous), time of collection, FiO2.
  4. Obtain read-back confirmation.
  5. Document: who was notified, time, read-back confirmed, in the patient record.

Notes for Shipboard Use

PLACEHOLDER

Motion & Vibration

Blood gas analysis is particularly sensitive to sample handling. In rough seas, brace the analyser securely and minimise sample exposure time. Air bubble introduction during ship motion is a significant risk — take extra care when expelling bubbles from the syringe.

Temperature Considerations

CG4+ cartridges require the same storage and equilibration as other i-STAT cartridges. Additionally, the blood sample itself is temperature-sensitive — do not expose to heat or direct sunlight between collection and analysis.

Documentation at Sea

Always record the sample type (arterial vs. venous), FiO2, ventilator settings if applicable, and the time between collection and analysis. These contextual details are essential for meaningful interpretation of blood gas results, especially when remote medical consultation is required via telemedicine.

Tip: In a mass casualty or resuscitation scenario, the CG4+ provides the most immediately actionable information about ventilation and perfusion status. Pair with CHEM8+ for a comprehensive metabolic and oxygenation picture.

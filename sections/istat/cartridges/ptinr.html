Purpose & Clinical Indications

PLACEHOLDER

What This Cartridge Tests

The PT/INR cartridge measures the Prothrombin Time (PT) in seconds and calculates the International Normalised Ratio (INR). PT/INR assesses the extrinsic and common coagulation pathways.

Clinical Indications

  • Monitoring warfarin (Coumadin) anticoagulation therapy
  • Pre-operative coagulation screening
  • Assessment of coagulopathy in trauma / massive transfusion
  • Liver function assessment (coagulation factor synthesis)
  • Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC) assessment
  • Monitoring vitamin K deficiency

Limitations & Interferences

Heparin at therapeutic levels may affect PT results. The i-STAT PT/INR is calibrated for warfarin monitoring — results in patients on direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs) such as rivaroxaban or apixaban may not be reliable. Severely lipaemic or haemolysed samples may interfere.

Caution — DOAC Patients: The i-STAT PT/INR cartridge is not validated for monitoring direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs). PT/INR results in DOAC patients should be interpreted with caution.

Storage & Handling

PLACEHOLDER

Storage Temperature

Refrigerated: 2–8 °C. Room temperature storage range and duration per manufacturer.

Shelf Life & Expiry

Check expiry date on each cartridge pouch. Once the foil pouch is opened, use promptly.

Lot Tracking

Record lot number upon receipt. New lots require liquid QC at all 3 levels before patient use.

Equilibration Before Use

Caution: Allow cartridge to equilibrate to room temperature (18–30 °C) for at least 5 minutes before use.

QC Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

  1. Verify EQC has passed today.
  2. Check the QC logbook — is liquid QC due for PT/INR this week?
  3. If due: run Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 liquid QC per QC procedures.
  4. Compare PT and INR results against lot-specific acceptable ranges.
  5. Record all results (PASS/FAIL) in the QC logbook.
  6. If any level fails, do NOT run patient samples on PT/INR until resolved.
  7. See QC Failure Troubleshooting for corrective actions.

Patient Sample Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

Pre-Analysis

  1. Confirm patient identity using two identifiers.
  2. Verify EQC passed and QC is current for PT/INR.
  3. Gather all supplies.
  4. Remove a PT/INR cartridge from its foil pouch; confirm room temperature and in-date.

Sample Collection

  1. Perform venepuncture using standard technique.
  2. Collect sample into the appropriate container (native or citrated per cartridge instructions).
  3. If using a syringe, mix gently if anticoagulant is present.
  4. Avoid prolonged tourniquet time (may activate coagulation).
Caution: For PT/INR testing, sample timing is critical. Analyse as soon as possible after collection — coagulation factors begin to degrade within minutes.

Running the Test

  1. Apply the blood sample to the PT/INR cartridge sample well — fill to the line.
  2. Close the cartridge cover firmly.
  3. Insert the cartridge into the i-STAT analyser.
  4. Enter patient ID and operator ID when prompted.
  5. Wait for the analysis to complete (~2 minutes).

Post-Analysis

  1. Read the PT (seconds) and INR (ratio) results on the display.
  2. Print the result ticket (if available).
  3. Dispose of the used cartridge in the sharps/biohazard container.
  4. Document results in the patient record — include the INR target range if the patient is on warfarin.
  5. Check for critical values and report immediately if present.

Critical Value Reporting

PLACEHOLDER

Critical values must be reported immediately to the responsible medical officer. An INR significantly above the therapeutic range carries a high risk of bleeding.

Reporting Procedure

  1. Identify the critical value on the result display.
  2. Immediately contact the responsible medical officer.
  3. Report: patient identity, INR value, PT value, current warfarin dose (if known), any signs of bleeding.
  4. Obtain read-back confirmation.
  5. Document notification in the patient record.

Notes for Shipboard Use

PLACEHOLDER

Motion & Vibration

PT/INR testing is less sensitive to motion than blood gas analysis, but the sample must still be handled carefully. Excessive agitation may activate coagulation factors prematurely.

Temperature Considerations

Standard cartridge storage and equilibration requirements apply. Ensure the medical centre refrigerator is functioning correctly, especially in tropical environments.

Documentation at Sea

For warfarin-monitored patients, document: INR result, warfarin dose, time since last dose, any concurrent medications that may interact with warfarin, and the dose adjustment decision. This documentation supports continuity of care and telemedicine consultation.

Tip: If a crew member requires ongoing warfarin monitoring at sea, establish a regular testing schedule and maintain a trend log of INR results and dose adjustments.

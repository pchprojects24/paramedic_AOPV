PT/INR

Rapid anticoagulation status for bleeding risk and warfarin monitoring.

Purpose

  • Prothrombin time and INR for patients on warfarin or with suspected coagulopathy.
  • Screening before invasive procedures or thrombolytic considerations when no lab support.

Supplies

  • PT/INR cartridge warmed to room temp.
  • Fresh whole blood: fingerstick (preferred) or venous sample free of heparin/citrate contamination.
  • Alcohol swab, lancet, gauze, timer, and labels.

Storage & Handling

  • Store at 2–8°C; label pouch with out-of-fridge date (typical room-temp limit 14 days; verify lot insert).
  • Warm sealed pouch 5–10 minutes; open only when ready to fill.
  • Discard damaged or damp pouches; avoid freezing.

QC Workflow

  1. Run liquid control(s) with each new lot/shipment or if analyzer prompts QC.
  2. Electronic simulator after transport or weekly if idle.
  3. Record lot numbers, ranges, and PASS/FAIL; quarantine lot if controls fail repeatedly.

Patient Sample Workflow

  1. Prepare cartridge and analyzer; have gauze ready.
  2. Fingerstick: clean, let dry, lance, wipe first drop, then allow a free-flowing second drop without squeezing.
  3. Fill cartridge promptly to the line (within 30 seconds of collection), close cap, insert, and start test.
  4. Venous sample: avoid heparinized lines; if using a line, discard a few mL first and test within minutes.
  5. Document INR/PT, time drawn, medications, and any bridging plan.

Common Errors & Fixes

  • Clotted or slow-flow fingerstick: Ensure warm finger, good perfusion, and prompt fill; redraw if clotted.
  • Heparin contamination (false high INR): Avoid sampling from heparinized lines or flush thoroughly and discard.
  • Underfill: Use a new cartridge and collect a fresh sample; fill in one motion.

Notes for Shipboard Use

  • Not validated for DOAC monitoring; interpret cautiously and consult higher care if uncertainty remains.
  • Repeat if clinical picture and result disagree; confirm ashore when possible.
  • Record result/time in the anticoagulation or casualty log for continuity during evacuations.

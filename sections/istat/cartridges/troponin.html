Purpose & Clinical Indications

PLACEHOLDER

What This Cartridge Tests

The Troponin I cartridge measures cardiac Troponin I (cTnI), a highly specific biomarker for myocardial cell injury. Elevated cTnI indicates damage to cardiac muscle tissue.

Clinical Indications

  • Assessment of acute coronary syndrome (ACS) — STEMI, NSTEMI
  • Evaluation of chest pain / suspected myocardial infarction (MI)
  • Monitoring myocardial injury in trauma
  • Assessment of myocarditis
  • Post-resuscitation myocardial assessment
  • Risk stratification in acute chest pain presentations
  • Serial testing to detect rising/falling troponin trends

Limitations & Interferences

  • A single negative troponin does NOT rule out MI — serial testing at 3–6 hour intervals is required.
  • Troponin may be elevated in non-cardiac conditions: renal failure, sepsis, PE, myocarditis, strenuous exercise.
  • The i-STAT cTnI is a conventional (not high-sensitivity) troponin assay — detection sensitivity differs from laboratory hs-cTnI/hs-cTnT assays.
  • Haemolysis, lipaemia, and HAMA (human anti-mouse antibodies) may interfere.
Critical Limitation: A negative i-STAT troponin result in the first few hours after symptom onset does NOT exclude myocardial infarction. Troponin levels may not rise above the detection threshold for 3–6 hours after myocardial injury. Always correlate with clinical presentation, ECG findings, and repeat testing.

Storage & Handling

PLACEHOLDER

Storage Temperature

Refrigerated: 2–8 °C. Room temperature storage range and duration per manufacturer instructions.

Shelf Life & Expiry

Check expiry date on each cartridge pouch. Once the foil pouch is opened, use promptly. The Troponin I cartridge may have a shorter shelf life than chemistry cartridges — verify.

Lot Tracking

Record lot number upon receipt. New lots require liquid QC at all 3 levels before patient use. Given the clinical significance of troponin results, meticulous lot tracking is essential.

Equilibration Before Use

Caution: Allow cartridge to equilibrate to room temperature (18–30 °C) for at least 5 minutes before use. Do not artificially warm.

QC Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

  1. Verify EQC has passed today.
  2. Check the QC logbook — is liquid QC due for Troponin I this week?
  3. If due: run Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 liquid QC per QC procedures.
  4. Compare cTnI results against lot-specific acceptable ranges.
  5. Record all results (PASS/FAIL) in the QC logbook.
  6. If any level fails, do NOT run patient samples on Troponin I until resolved.
  7. See QC Failure Troubleshooting for corrective actions.
Note: The Troponin I cartridge has a longer analysis time (~10 minutes vs ~2 minutes for other cartridges). This also applies during QC testing — plan accordingly.

Patient Sample Workflow

PLACEHOLDER

Pre-Analysis

  1. Confirm patient identity using two identifiers.
  2. Verify that EQC has passed and QC is current for Troponin I.
  3. Gather all supplies (see checklist above).
  4. Remove a Troponin I cartridge from its foil pouch; confirm room temperature and in-date.
  5. Note the time of symptom onset and time of blood draw — both are clinically important.

Sample Collection

  1. Perform venepuncture using standard technique.
  2. Collect venous whole blood into a lithium heparin syringe or tube.
  3. Mix gently by inversion.
  4. Analyse as soon as possible after collection.

Running the Test

  1. Apply the blood sample to the Troponin I cartridge sample well — fill to the line.
  2. Close the cartridge cover firmly.
  3. Insert the cartridge into the i-STAT analyser.
  4. Enter patient ID and operator ID when prompted.
  5. Wait for the analysis to complete — approximately 10 minutes.
  6. Do NOT remove the cartridge during the analysis cycle.
Important: The Troponin I cartridge takes approximately 10 minutes to produce a result. Do not disturb the analyser during this time. Plan clinical workflow accordingly.

Post-Analysis

  1. Read the cTnI result on the display.
  2. Print the result ticket (if available).
  3. Dispose of the used cartridge in the sharps/biohazard container.
  4. Document: cTnI result, time of symptom onset, time of blood draw, time of result.
  5. Interpret in context — a single result is insufficient for diagnosis.
  6. Plan serial testing at 3–6 hour intervals if initial result is negative but clinical suspicion persists.
  7. Report any elevated result immediately.

Critical Value Reporting

PLACEHOLDER

Any elevated troponin result must be reported immediately to the responsible medical officer. An elevated cTnI in the context of chest pain may indicate an acute myocardial infarction requiring urgent intervention.

Troponin I Critical Threshold

AnalyteCritical Threshold
cTnIAny value above the 99th percentile URL: PLACEHOLDER ng/mL

Reporting Procedure

  1. Identify the elevated cTnI result on the display.
  2. Immediately contact the responsible medical officer.
  3. Report: patient identity, cTnI result, time of symptom onset, time of blood draw, ECG findings (if available).
  4. Obtain read-back confirmation.
  5. Document: who was notified, time of notification, read-back confirmed, in the patient record.
  6. Prepare for serial testing at 3–6 hours to assess trend (rising, falling, stable).

Notes for Shipboard Use

PLACEHOLDER

Motion & Vibration

The 10-minute analysis time means the analyser must remain stable for a longer period than other cartridge types. Secure the analyser on a stable, non-vibrating surface. In heavy weather, consider timing the test for a period of relative calm if possible.

Temperature Considerations

Standard cartridge storage and equilibration requirements apply. The Troponin I cartridge may be more sensitive to temperature extremes — verify manufacturer specifications.

Documentation at Sea

Cardiac events at sea require detailed documentation for medical evacuation planning and telemedicine consultation. Record: all troponin results with timestamps, ECG findings, vital signs trend, symptom timeline, and any treatments administered. This information is critical for the receiving facility.

Tip: If a suspected cardiac event occurs at sea and the initial troponin is negative, do not be reassured. Plan serial testing at 3 and 6 hours. Begin telemedicine consultation early and prepare for potential medical evacuation based on the full clinical picture, not just the troponin result alone.
Stock Consideration: Troponin I cartridges are used less frequently than CHEM8+ or CG4+ but are critically important when needed. Maintain adequate stock and monitor expiry dates carefully — expiry before use is a common issue with infrequently used cartridges.

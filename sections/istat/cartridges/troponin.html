Troponin I

Cardiac marker for chest pain/ACS triage when no lab support is available.

Purpose

  • Detects myocardial injury; use with history/ECG and repeat testing for serial change.
  • Supports thrombolytic/evacuation decisions when timelines are tight at sea.

Supplies

  • Troponin I cartridge warmed to room temp.
  • Fresh lithium heparin whole blood (venous preferred) 2–3 mL; avoid hemolysis.
  • Alcohol swab, gauze, labels, timer, and PPE.

Storage & Handling

  • Store refrigerated at 2–8°C; mark out-of-fridge date (typical room-temp limit 14 days; verify lot insert).
  • Warm sealed pouch 5–10 minutes; keep level and dry.
  • Discard if foil is damaged or pouch is wet/condensed.

QC Workflow

  1. Run liquid controls with each new lot or shipment and whenever prompted by QC schedule.
  2. Electronic simulator after transport or weekly if idle.
  3. Document lot numbers, ranges, and outcomes; investigate repeat failures.

Patient Sample Workflow

  1. Warm cartridge; confirm analyzer ready and patient ID available.
  2. Draw lithium heparin blood, avoiding hemolysis; gently invert to mix.
  3. Fill cartridge to the line without bubbles, close the cap, and insert within 30 seconds.
  4. Start test; expect result in ~10 minutes. Note the time as “T0 troponin.”
  5. Repeat per protocol (e.g., at 3 hours) if suspicion remains despite an initial negative.
  6. Print/log results with ECG interpretation and symptom onset time.

Common Errors & Fixes

  • Hemolysis: Use a clean venipuncture, avoid small needles and forceful draws.
  • Underfill: Recollect and refill a new cartridge promptly.
  • Early negative with high suspicion: Repeat at 3 hours or sooner if clinical change; manage per ACS protocols.

Notes for Shipboard Use

  • Do not use a single negative result to rule out MI in the first hours of chest pain.
  • Pair with ECG monitoring, nitro/ASA protocols, and evacuation planning.
  • Keep a small number of troponin cartridges at room temp for rapid response; rotate stock before 14-day limit.

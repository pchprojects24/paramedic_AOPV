QC Overview & Requirements

PLACEHOLDER

Purpose of Quality Control

QC ensures the i-STAT analyser and cartridges produce accurate, reliable results. It detects systematic errors before they affect patient care.

Regulatory Requirements

Applicable standards: ISO 22870 (POCT quality and competence), CLIA requirements, organisational POCT policy, manufacturer recommendations.

Types of QC

  • Electronic QC (EQC): Tests the analyser's electronic circuits using a reusable simulator.
  • Liquid QC: Tests the full analytical process (cartridge + analyser) using control solutions with known analyte concentrations.

Responsibility

Who is responsible for performing QC, reviewing QC results, and taking corrective action. Role of the POCT coordinator.

Electronic QC (EQC)

PLACEHOLDER

What EQC Tests

The Electronic Simulator verifies: signal acquisition circuits, amplification paths, analog-to-digital conversion, microprocessor calculations, and detection thresholds.

EQC Procedure

  1. Ensure the analyser is powered on and at the main menu.
  2. Insert the Electronic Simulator into the cartridge port.
  3. Wait for the test cycle to complete (~60 seconds).
  4. Read the result: PASS or FAIL.
  5. Remove the simulator; return to protective case.
  6. Record the result, simulator lot number, date, time, and operator ID in the QC logbook.

EQC Frequency

  • Daily — before the first patient test of the day.
  • After any battery change.
  • After any suspected analyser fault.
  • After the analyser has been dropped or exposed to extreme conditions.

EQC Simulator Lot Management

Lot number tracking, expiry, storage conditions, replacement process.

Caution: An EQC PASS does not verify cartridge integrity. Liquid QC is still required to confirm the full analytical process.

Liquid QC Controls

PLACEHOLDER

Overview

Liquid QC materials are aqueous solutions with known concentrations of the analytes measured by each cartridge type. Three levels bracket the clinically relevant range.

Level 1 — Low / Normal Range

Verifies performance at the low end of the reportable range. Control material identification, storage, handling, expiry management.

  1. Remove Level 1 ampoule from refrigerator; equilibrate to room temp (5+ minutes).
  2. Mix by gentle inversion (do not vortex).
  3. Open ampoule per manufacturer instructions.
  4. Apply control material to the cartridge sample well using the supplied applicator.
  5. Insert cartridge; wait for results.
  6. Compare each analyte result against the Level 1 acceptable range insert.
  7. Record PASS or FAIL for each analyte.

Level 2 — Mid / Normal Range

Verifies performance at the mid-range of the reportable range.

  1. Repeat Level 1 procedure using Level 2 control material.
  2. Compare to Level 2 ranges; record results.

Level 3 — High / Abnormal Range

Verifies performance at the high end, including critical value ranges.

  1. Repeat Level 1 procedure using Level 3 control material.
  2. Compare to Level 3 ranges; record results.

Control Material Management

  • Storage temperature requirements
  • Expiry date tracking and disposal
  • Lot number documentation
  • Opened vs. unopened shelf life
  • Ordering and supply chain considerations

Standard QC Schedule

QC TypeFrequencyTrigger Events
Electronic QC (EQC) Daily (minimum) Battery change, suspected fault, drop/impact, extreme environmental exposure
Liquid QC — All 3 Levels Weekly (minimum) per cartridge type New cartridge lot, new QC lot, after analyser service/repair
Liquid QC — New Lot Before first patient use of new cartridge lot Receipt of new cartridge shipment

Enhanced Schedule (High-Usage Periods)

During mass casualty events, extended operations, or high test volume periods, consider increasing QC frequency (e.g., liquid QC every 24 hours).

Note: Local POCT policy may require more frequent QC than the minimums listed above. Always follow the most stringent applicable requirement.

QC Failure Troubleshooting

PLACEHOLDER

Critical: Do NOT release any patient results for a cartridge type that has failed QC until the failure is resolved and QC passes.

Step-by-Step Failure Response

  1. Record the failure: cartridge type, level, analyte(s) out of range, date/time, operator.
  2. Verify QC material: correct level, not expired, properly stored, properly mixed.
  3. Check cartridge: correct type, not expired, properly equilibrated, lot number matches.
  4. Re-run the failed level using a new cartridge from the same lot.
  5. If PASS — record corrective action; resume patient testing.
  6. If FAIL again — try a cartridge from a different lot (if available).
  7. If still FAIL — run EQC to rule out analyser fault.
  8. If EQC FAIL — analyser may need service. Do NOT use for patient testing.
  9. If EQC PASS but liquid QC still FAIL — suspect QC material or cartridge lot issue.
  10. Contact POCT coordinator for guidance.

CauseSignsAction
Expired QC materialAll levels fail on all cartridge typesCheck expiry; use fresh material.

Lockout Procedures

PLACEHOLDER

When to Lock Out the Analyser

  • EQC fails after cleaning and repeat testing.
  • Liquid QC fails on all cartridge types after full troubleshooting.
  • Analyser shows persistent error codes.
  • Physical damage to the analyser.

Lockout Process

  1. Physically label the analyser as "OUT OF SERVICE — DO NOT USE".
  2. Record the lockout in the QC logbook with date, time, reason, and operator.
  3. Notify the POCT coordinator and medical officer.
  4. Arrange repair or replacement.
  5. Before returning to service, pass EQC + liquid QC at all 3 levels on all cartridge types.

When to Lock Out a Cartridge Lot

  • Liquid QC repeatedly fails on a specific lot.
  • Known manufacturer recall or advisory for that lot.
  • Expired lot.

Cartridge Lot Lockout Process

  1. Segregate the affected lot from usable stock.
  2. Label clearly as "DO NOT USE".
  3. Record in QC logbook.
  4. Contact supplier / manufacturer if lot defect is suspected.
  5. Dispose per organisational policy.

Documentation & POCT Coordinator Reporting

PLACEHOLDER

QC Logbook Requirements

Every QC event (EQC and liquid) must be recorded with:

  • Date and time
  • Operator name / ID
  • QC type (EQC or Liquid, with level)
  • Cartridge type and lot number
  • QC material lot number and expiry
  • Result for each analyte (value + PASS/FAIL)
  • Corrective actions (if any)

Monthly / Quarterly Reporting

Summary reports to the POCT coordinator should include:

  • Number of QC events performed (EQC + liquid)
  • Pass/fail rates per cartridge type
  • Corrective actions taken
  • Lockout events
  • Cartridge inventory and expiry status
  • Training / competency updates

Audit Trail

The QC logbook serves as the primary audit document. Ensure legibility, completeness, no white-out corrections (use single-line strikethrough with initials and date for corrections).

Tip: Keep a photocopy or digital backup of the QC logbook pages. In the maritime environment, water damage to paper records is a real risk.

Quality Control

QC keeps results reliable and may lock out testing if overdue.

When QC is required

  • New shipment or lot number of cartridges (all levels recommended before patient use).
  • Weekly for any lot in use, or per the ship’s POCT/QC schedule.
  • After temperature excursions, dropping the analyzer, rough transit, or maintenance.
  • When prompted by analyzer lockouts or after major time/date changes.

Electronic simulator (functional check)

  1. Allow analyzer to reach room temp; connect the electronic simulator to the cartridge port.
  2. Select Electronic Simulator or follow on-screen prompts; verify PASS.
  3. Record date/time, analyzer ID, and PASS/FAIL in the QC log; investigate any FAIL before patient testing.

Liquid QC

  1. Keep cartridge and QC ampoule sealed until both reach room temp (about 10 minutes out of the fridge).
  2. Mix QC ampoule gently, open, and use the provided dropper/syringe to fill the cartridge exactly to the fill line without bubbles.
  3. Insert cartridge, select QC mode if offered, and run like a patient sample.
  4. Compare results to the manufacturer’s acceptable range for that lot; document lot numbers, operator, and outcome.

If QC fails

  • Repeat with a new cartridge and fresh QC sample after confirming temperature.
  • Re-run the electronic simulator; inspect cartridge port and battery charge.
  • If repeat failures persist, quarantine suspect lot, switch to another lot, and notify the chain for replacement and biomedical support.

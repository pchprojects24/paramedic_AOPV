i-STAT Cartridges

Handling, storage, and selection tips for common shipboard cartridges.

Storage & handling

  • Keep sealed pouches refrigerated (2–8°C). Track “out of fridge” date on the pouch; typical room-temp limit is 14 days—follow the kit insert for the exact lot.
  • Warm sealed cartridge to room temperature for at least 5–10 minutes before opening; avoid condensation or hand-warming.
  • Do not freeze. Discard pouches with damaged foil, moisture, or expired dates.

General workflow

  1. Power on, confirm QC status, and stage labels/patient identifiers.
  2. Open pouch immediately before use; keep cartridge level.
  3. Fill smoothly to the line without bubbles using fresh whole blood (heparinized unless otherwise specified).
  4. Close the cap, insert into analyzer promptly (within 30 seconds for most cartridges), and follow prompts.
  5. Print/log results in the casualty record and annotate cartridge lot, time, and operator.

What to pick

  • CHEM8+ — electrolytes, renal function, glucose, hematocrit/hemoglobin, ionized calcium.
  • CG4+ — blood gas and lactate for respiratory failure or sepsis/poor perfusion.
  • PT/INR — anticoagulation status for bleeding risk or warfarin monitoring.
  • Troponin I — cardiac biomarker for chest pain/ACS evaluation.

Shipboard notes

  • Use a small insulated container for transporting cartridges to casualty areas; keep pouches dry.
  • Record fridge temps twice daily; stash a few room-temp cartridges for rapid use but rotate before the 14-day limit.
  • Dispose of used cartridges as biohazard waste; clean analyzer surfaces after each use.

Clearing In

Step-by-step guide for a paramedic joining the ship

This page covers the complete clearing-in process for a paramedic posting to an AOPV. It includes Day 1 tasks, first week and first month milestones, key personnel, systems access, and inventory handover procedures. Purpose

Placeholder The clearing-in process ensures a smooth transition between outgoing and incoming paramedics, maintains continuity of medical care, and verifies accountability for all medical supplies, equipment, and controlled substances.

Timeline

Placeholder Ideal clearing-in period with overlap between outgoing and incoming paramedic. Minimum recommended overlap period for proper handover.

Key Principle

Placeholder Never sign for inventory, controlled substances, or equipment without conducting a thorough joint count and inspection with the outgoing paramedic and a witness.

  1. Placeholder Report to Coxswain and present posting message.
  2. Placeholder Meet outgoing paramedic for initial orientation.
  3. Placeholder Tour of sickbay: layout, equipment locations, storage areas.
  4. Placeholder Tour of ship: CCS locations, emergency equipment stations, key compartments.
  5. Placeholder Receive keys (sickbay, controlled substance locker, medical stores).
  6. Placeholder Witness controlled substance count with outgoing paramedic.
  7. Placeholder Review current patient load and any active medical cases.
  8. Placeholder Receive cabin assignment and bunk allocation.
  9. Placeholder Complete ship's joining routine (ID card, meal card, duty watch assignment).
  10. Placeholder Receive ship's Standing Orders and read and sign.

Critical: Controlled substance handover must be conducted with a formal count, witnessed by a third party (typically the Coxswain or designated officer), and documented with signatures from both outgoing and incoming paramedics and the witness.

Controlled Substance Handover

  1. Placeholder Schedule formal handover with outgoing paramedic and witness.
  2. Placeholder Count every controlled substance item against the register.
  3. Placeholder Verify expiry dates of all controlled substances.
  4. Placeholder Review controlled substance usage logs.
  5. Placeholder Both paramedics and witness sign the handover form.
  6. Placeholder Transfer safe / locker key custody.
  7. Placeholder Change combination if applicable.

General Inventory Handover

  1. Placeholder Walk through all storage locations.
  2. Placeholder Count and verify against master inventory list.
  3. Placeholder Note any shortages, damaged items, or pending orders.
  4. Placeholder Review expiry dates — identify items expiring within 90 days.
  5. Placeholder Sign inventory acceptance form.

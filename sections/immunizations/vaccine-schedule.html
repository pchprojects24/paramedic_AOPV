CAF Vaccine Schedule

Standard immunizations administered to Canadian Armed Forces members. Always confirm current CF H Svcs Gp directives — schedules may change with new NACI recommendations or operational requirements.

Routine CAF Immunizations

Every CAF member must maintain currency on the following vaccines as part of their baseline immunization profile:

  • Tetanus–Diphtheria–Pertussis (Tdap/Td): Tdap once in adulthood, then Td booster every 10 years. Ensure wound-related boosters are given if >5 years since last dose.
  • Influenza (Inactivated): Annually, typically Oct–Nov. All CAF members are required to receive the seasonal flu vaccine each year.
  • Hepatitis A: Two-dose series (0, 6–12 months). Required for all CAF members.
  • Hepatitis B: Three-dose series (0, 1, 6 months). Anti-HBs titre checked post-series; non-responders may need a second series.
  • Hepatitis A+B Combined (Twinrix): Three-dose series (0, 1, 6 months) or accelerated schedule (0, 7d, 21d + 12-month booster). Often used in lieu of separate Hep A and Hep B series.
  • MMR (Measles–Mumps–Rubella): Two documented doses or serologic proof of immunity. Born before 1970 generally considered immune to measles.
  • Polio (IPV): Primary series plus one adult booster if not previously received.
  • Varicella: Two doses if no documented history of disease or serology negative. Check titre if uncertain.
  • Meningococcal (Conjugate — Men-C-ACYW): One dose; required for all CAF personnel.

Deployment / Operational Immunizations

Additional vaccines may be required based on area of operations, threat assessment, and CF H Svcs Gp directives:

  • Yellow Fever: Single dose (live vaccine); required for travel to endemic regions in Africa and South America. Valid for life per WHO (confirm CAF policy). Must be administered at a designated Yellow Fever Vaccination Centre.
  • Typhoid: Injectable (Vi polysaccharide) — one dose, revaccinate every 3 years if risk continues. Oral (Ty21a) — 4 capsules over 7 days, revaccinate every 7 years.
  • Japanese Encephalitis: Two-dose series (0, 28 days) for deployment to endemic areas in Asia/Western Pacific.
  • Rabies (Pre-exposure): Three-dose series (0, 7, 21–28 days) for personnel at risk. Post-exposure still requires additional doses even if pre-vaccinated.
  • Cholera / ETEC (Dukoral): Two oral doses (1–6 weeks apart) for deployments to endemic areas. Booster after 2 years.
  • Anthrax: As directed by Surg Gen for specific threat environments. Multi-dose intramuscular series.
  • Smallpox: As directed for specific threat environments. Live vaccine with significant contraindications.
  • COVID-19: As per current CF H Svcs Gp directives and NACI recommendations.

Documentation Requirements

  • All immunizations must be recorded in the member's CF 2034 (Immunization Record) and the CFHIS electronic health record.
  • Lot number, site of injection, route, dose, date, and immunizer identity must be documented for every vaccine administered.
  • Adverse events must be reported using the AEFI (Adverse Event Following Immunization) form and entered into CFHIS.
  • Members should carry their yellow WHO International Certificate of Vaccination for international travel.

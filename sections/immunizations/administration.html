Vaccine Administration Procedures

Step-by-step procedures for safe vaccine administration. Always confirm that a primary immunizer has authorized the immunization before proceeding.

Pre-Immunization Steps

  1. Verify authorization: Confirm the primary immunizer has prescribed the vaccine(s) or that a valid medical directive/standing order covers this immunization.
  2. Screen the patient: Review the pre-immunization checklist — ask about allergies (especially to egg, latex, or vaccine components), current illness, pregnancy, immunosuppression, and history of adverse reactions to vaccines.
  3. Obtain informed consent: Explain what vaccine is being given, common side effects, and when to seek medical attention. Document consent.
  4. Review the CF 2034: Check the member's immunization record for previous doses, intervals, and any outstanding vaccines.
  5. Verify the vaccine: Confirm correct product, lot number, expiry date, and that the cold chain has been maintained. Inspect for discolouration, particulates, or damage.

Injection Technique — Intramuscular (IM)

Most CAF vaccines are given IM. The preferred site for adults is the deltoid muscle.

  1. Position the patient seated with the arm relaxed at their side.
  2. Identify the deltoid: thickest part of the muscle, approximately 2–3 finger-widths below the acromion process.
  3. Clean the site with an alcohol swab and allow to air-dry.
  4. Use a 22–25 gauge needle, 25 mm (1 inch) for most adults. Use 38 mm (1.5 inch) for larger patients or deeper muscle mass.
  5. Insert the needle at a 90-degree angle to the skin in a quick, dart-like motion.
  6. Aspirating before injection is not recommended for IM vaccine administration (per current NACI/WHO guidance).
  7. Inject the vaccine at a steady rate.
  8. Withdraw the needle and apply light pressure with a dry swab. Apply an adhesive bandage.

Injection Technique — Subcutaneous (SC)

Some live vaccines (e.g., MMR, varicella) are given SC.

  1. Preferred site: posterior aspect of the upper arm (triceps area) or anterolateral thigh.
  2. Use a 25–27 gauge needle, 16 mm (⅝ inch).
  3. Pinch a fold of skin and insert the needle at a 45-degree angle.
  4. Inject slowly, withdraw, and apply light pressure.

Multiple Vaccines at One Visit

  • Different vaccines can be given at the same visit using different injection sites.
  • If two IM injections are given in the same limb, separate the sites by at least 2.5 cm (1 inch).
  • Document the specific site used for each vaccine (e.g., "left deltoid" vs. "right deltoid").
  • Live injectable vaccines not given on the same day should be separated by at least 28 days.

Post-Immunization

  1. Instruct the patient to remain in the observation area for a minimum of 15 minutes (30 minutes if history of allergic reactions or receiving a new vaccine).
  2. Advise on common side effects: soreness at the injection site, mild fever, fatigue — typically resolving within 24–48 hours.
  3. Counsel to seek medical attention for: persistent high fever, difficulty breathing, widespread rash, or swelling of the face/throat.
  4. Complete all documentation: CF 2034, CFHIS entry, lot number, site, route, dose, date, and immunizer identity.
  5. Dispose of sharps in the sharps container immediately — never recap needles.

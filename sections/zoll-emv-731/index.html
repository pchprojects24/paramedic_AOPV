Ventilator — ZOLL EMV+ 731

Quick-start guide and essential operating instructions for the ZOLL EMV+ 731 portable ventilator. This device provides invasive and non-invasive ventilation for transport and field use. Follow local protocols and medical direction at all times.

Essential startup procedure

  1. Power and gas supply: Connect the ventilator to a high-pressure oxygen source (cylinder or wall outlet) using the DISS fitting. Confirm the supply pressure gauge reads within the acceptable range (typically 40–87 psi). Install a fully charged battery or connect to AC/DC power.
  2. Circuit assembly: Attach a new single-patient ventilator circuit to the patient outlet. Connect the exhalation valve, proximal flow sensor (if used), and in-line filter. Perform a brief occlusion test: block the patient end of the circuit and confirm the device detects the occlusion and pressure rises.
  3. Power on and self-test: Press the power button. Allow the device to complete its automatic self-test. Verify that no error codes or alarms appear. Confirm the date, time, and oxygen source indicator are correct.
  4. Select ventilation mode: Choose the appropriate mode for the patient:
    • A/C (Assist/Control): Set rate, tidal volume, and FiO2. The patient can trigger additional breaths above the set rate.
    • SIMV: Set rate, tidal volume, pressure support, and FiO2. Allows spontaneous breaths between mandatory breaths.
    • CPAP/PSV: Set PEEP, pressure support, and FiO2 for spontaneously breathing patients.
  5. Set parameters: Dial in the prescribed settings:
    • Tidal volume: Typically 6–8 mL/kg ideal body weight for adults.
    • Respiratory rate: Typically 12–20 breaths/min for adults.
    • FiO2: Start at 100% and titrate down per SpO2/blood gas targets.
    • PEEP: Typically 5 cmH2O to start; adjust per clinical need.
  6. Set alarm limits: Adjust high and low pressure alarms, high and low minute volume alarms, and apnea alarm settings appropriate to the patient size and condition.
  7. Connect to patient: Attach the circuit to the endotracheal tube, tracheostomy, or non-invasive mask. Confirm chest rise, auscultate bilateral breath sounds, and verify waveforms and exhaled tidal volume on the display.

You are now ventilating. Monitor the patient continuously, check SpO2 and EtCO2 if available, and reassess settings frequently.

Adjusting Settings During Ventilation

  1. Press the parameter you want to change on the display or use the selector knob.
  2. Dial in the new value and confirm. The change takes effect on the next breath cycle.
  3. Monitor the patient response over several breath cycles before making further adjustments.

Switching Ventilation Modes

  1. Press the Mode key to access the mode selection screen.
  2. Select the desired mode and set the required parameters before confirming.
  3. The ventilator will transition to the new mode. Verify adequate ventilation immediately after switching.

Oxygen Management

  • Monitor the oxygen supply pressure gauge regularly. Replace cylinders before pressure drops below the low-supply threshold.
  • Titrate FiO2 down from 100% as soon as clinically appropriate to conserve oxygen during prolonged transport.
  • The device will alarm when gas supply is critically low. Have a backup BVM immediately accessible.

Silencing Alarms

  • Press the Alarm Silence button to temporarily mute an active alarm (typically 60 seconds).
  • Identify and correct the cause of the alarm before it reactivates.
  • Never leave alarms silenced for extended periods without addressing the root cause.

Battery and Power

  • Connect to AC/DC power whenever possible to preserve battery life during extended use.
  • Monitor the battery indicator on the display. Swap to a charged battery before full depletion.
  • The device will provide an audible and visual low-battery alarm. Connect to external power immediately when this occurs.

High Pressure Alarm

  • Check for kinks, water accumulation, or secretions in the circuit and ET tube.
  • Suction the patient if secretions are suspected.
  • Assess for bronchospasm, pneumothorax, or patient biting the ET tube (use a bite block).
  • If the alarm persists, reduce tidal volume or peak pressure settings and reassess.

Low Pressure / Disconnect Alarm

  • Check all circuit connections from the ventilator outlet to the patient.
  • Inspect the circuit for leaks, disconnected tubing, or a deflated ET tube cuff.
  • Reinflate the cuff and recheck. If the leak persists, replace the circuit or consider reintubation.

Low Minute Volume / Apnea Alarm

  • Assess the patient for respiratory depression or arrest.
  • Check that the flow sensor is clean, connected, and not obstructed by moisture.
  • Verify ventilator settings are appropriate and mode is correct for patient status.
  • If using CPAP/PSV and the patient is not triggering, switch to A/C mode.

Low Gas Supply Alarm

  • Check the cylinder pressure gauge and replace the cylinder immediately if low.
  • Ensure the DISS connection is tight and not leaking.
  • If no gas supply is available, disconnect the patient and ventilate manually with a BVM until supply is restored.

Device Failure / Will Not Start

  • Confirm the battery is installed and charged or AC/DC power is connected.
  • Try a power reset: turn off, wait 10 seconds, and turn back on.
  • If the device fails self-test or displays a persistent error, do not use. Switch to manual ventilation (BVM) and contact biomedical support.

Critical: Always have a bag-valve-mask (BVM) immediately accessible as a backup. If the ventilator fails for any reason, disconnect and ventilate manually without delay.

After Each Patient

  • Discard the single-patient circuit, exhalation valve, filters, and any contaminated components.
  • Wipe down the exterior of the ventilator with approved disinfectant. Avoid fluid ingress into ports and connectors.
  • Clean the flow sensor per manufacturer instructions or replace if disposable.

Routine Maintenance

  • Charge the battery fully after each use; test battery runtime monthly.
  • Inspect the oxygen DISS connection and supply hose for wear or damage.
  • Run the built-in self-test before each deployment and after transport.
  • Keep spare circuits, filters, exhalation valves, and a BVM packed with the ventilator at all times.

Consumables Checklist

  • Single-patient ventilator circuits (adult and pediatric sizes).
  • Exhalation valves and in-line bacterial/viral filters.
  • Proximal flow sensors (if disposable type).
  • Oxygen supply hose and DISS fittings.
  • Spare charged battery pack.
  • Backup BVM with mask (adult and pediatric).

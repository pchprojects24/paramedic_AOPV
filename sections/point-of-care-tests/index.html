Point-of-Care Tests (Non-iSTAT)

Rapid diagnostic tests performed at the bedside or sickbay

These are the non-iSTAT point-of-care tests (POCT) carried aboard. Each test has its own dedicated page covering procedure, interpretation, QC, and storage requirements. Content pending
What is POCT? Point-of-Care Testing refers to medical diagnostic tests performed near the patient rather than in a centralized laboratory. POCT enables rapid clinical decisions by providing results within minutes. These tests use self-contained kits that require minimal training and equipment, making them ideal for shipboard use where laboratory access is not available.
Quality Control Reminder All POCT kits require regular QC checks, proper storage, and expiry monitoring. Refer to each test's individual page for specific requirements. Document all QC results and patient test results per standing orders.

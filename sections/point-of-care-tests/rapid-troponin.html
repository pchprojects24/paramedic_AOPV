Rapid Troponin Test (Cardiac Troponin)

Rapid qualitative or semi-quantitative detection of cardiac troponin I/T

This page covers the rapid cardiac troponin test carried aboard for point-of-care evaluation of suspected acute coronary syndrome. All sections below are framework headings for AI (Codex) content population. Content pending
Critical: A negative rapid troponin does NOT rule out myocardial infarction. Serial testing and clinical correlation are essential. Always initiate ACS protocol regardless of POCT result if clinical suspicion is high.

Clinical Indications

Placeholder When to perform this test:

  • Chest pain suggestive of cardiac origin
  • Suspected acute coronary syndrome (ACS)
  • Unexplained dyspnea with cardiac risk factors
  • Post-resuscitation assessment
  • Atypical presentations: jaw pain, left arm pain, epigastric pain with risk factors

Important: Do not delay treatment of suspected ACS while waiting for troponin result. Initiate ACS protocol simultaneously.
  1. Placeholder Verify test kit is within expiry and packaging is intact.
  2. Placeholder Don appropriate PPE (gloves at minimum).
  3. Placeholder Allow test device to reach room temperature if refrigerated.
  4. Placeholder Prepare blood collection supplies (fingerstick or venipuncture).
  5. Placeholder Collect blood sample per manufacturer instructions.
  6. Placeholder Apply specified volume of blood to sample well.
  7. Placeholder Add buffer drops as specified by manufacturer.
  8. Placeholder Start timer immediately.
  9. Placeholder Read result at specified time (do not read after maximum time).
  10. Placeholder Verify control line is present.
  11. Placeholder Record result immediately.
  12. Placeholder Dispose of all materials appropriately (sharps in sharps container).

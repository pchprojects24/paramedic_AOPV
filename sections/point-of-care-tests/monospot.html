Clinical Indications

Placeholder When to perform this test:

  • Sore throat unresponsive to antibiotics
  • Pharyngitis with significant lymphadenopathy
  • Fatigue, malaise, and pharyngitis lasting >1 week
  • Fever with splenomegaly or hepatomegaly
  • Atypical lymphocytes on differential (if available)
  • Negative rapid strep with persistent pharyngitis symptoms
  • Additional indications to be added

When NOT to Use

Placeholder Age considerations (less reliable in children <12), timing (may be negative in first week of illness).

Test Limitations

  • Placeholder May be negative in first 1-2 weeks of illness (up to 25% false negative in week 1).
  • Placeholder Less reliable in children under 12 years of age.
  • Placeholder Does not confirm EBV specifically (other infections can cause heterophile antibodies).
  • Placeholder Heterophile antibodies may persist for months after resolution.
  • Placeholder Additional limitations to be added.

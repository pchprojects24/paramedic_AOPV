Suction — Laerdal LSU

Portable suction unit with rechargeable battery for airway clearance and secretion removal.

Quick start

  1. Inspect the canister, lid, tubing, filter, and catheter; ensure the battery has charge or connect to AC/vehicle power.
  2. Assemble the canister and tubing; confirm the hydrophobic filter is seated and the canister lid seals.
  3. Turn the unit on, set the vacuum regulator (e.g., adult ~300 mmHg, pediatric 80–120, neonate 60–80), and occlude the tubing to verify suction.
  4. Suction in short passes (<10 seconds), watching the gauge and patient response.
  5. After use, turn off, dispose of contents/consumables appropriately, and clean the unit.

Safety: Limit each suction pass to no more than 10 seconds in adults (shorter in pediatric/neonatal patients). Pre-oxygenate before suctioning intubated patients. Monitor heart rate and SpO2 during the procedure and stop if bradycardia or desaturation occurs.

Common functions

  • Operate from internal battery, AC, or vehicle power; monitor the charge indicator.
  • Adjust vacuum with the front regulator knob to match the patient category.
  • Swap between reusable and disposable canister configurations as supplied.

Troubleshooting

  • No or low suction: Check for kinks/occlusion, replace a saturated filter, reseat the canister lid/gasket, and verify tubing connections.
  • Low battery: Connect to AC/vehicle power and charge; replace the battery if run time is short.
  • Continuous alarm or leak: Inspect the canister seal and filter; replace consumables and retry.

Care & consumables

  • Hydrophobic filters and disposable canisters are single-use; replace after each patient.
  • Clean and disinfect exterior surfaces after use; avoid fluid ingress into the pump housing.
  • Charge the battery monthly if stored, and run a function check before deployment.

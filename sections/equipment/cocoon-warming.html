Cocoon Warming Device

Forced-air patient warmer (e.g., Cocoon CWS series) for perioperative or hypothermia prevention.

Quick start (under 2 minutes)

  1. Inspect the unit (power cord, hose, filter, and blanket) and place it on a stable surface with vents clear; connect to mains power.
  2. Select the correct single-patient blanket; connect the hose firmly to the device and blanket port with perforations facing the patient.
  3. Power on and choose a temperature setting (Ambient/Low/Medium/High). Allow airflow to warm before applying to the patient.
  4. Position the blanket so air outlets are not occluded; keep the patient’s face clear and monitor temperature/skin checks regularly.
  5. When finished, use standby/off, disconnect the hose, and let the unit cool before storage.

Common functions

  • Pre-warm a blanket on Ambient/Low before patient arrival.
  • Adjust temperature during use with the panel controls; use High only for short periods under clinical supervision.
  • Use standby to pause airflow briefly without powering down.
  • Check and seat the hose/blanket ports if alarms sound or airflow drops.

Troubleshooting

  • Low airflow/occlusion: Look for kinks, blocked blanket perforations, or a saturated filter; reseat the hose and clear obstructions.
  • Over-temperature alarm: Remove blanket from the patient, switch to Ambient, and inspect for blocked outlets or clogged filters.
  • No heat/power: Confirm mains power, reset any tripped circuit breaker/fuse, and check the power cord connection.
  • Unusual odor: Stop use, inspect/replace the filter, and request service if odor persists.

Care & consumables

  • Use single-patient warming blankets; dispose of after use.
  • Wipe exterior surfaces with a mild detergent; keep vents and filters dry.
  • Replace inlet filters on schedule or when prompted by alarms/inspection.
